Rice Farming Guide

1. Introduction to Bajra Farming

Bajra, or Pearl Millet, is a drought-resistant cereal crop grown in arid and semi-arid regions. It is a staple food for millions and is rich in iron, fiber, and protein. Bajra is used for human consumption, fodder, and in alcoholic beverages.

2. Suitable Climate and Soil

Climate:

  • Highly drought-tolerant; thrives in arid and semi-arid climates.
  • Ideal temperature: 25°C to 35°C.
  • Low rainfall requirement: 300-500 mm annually.

Soil:

  • Well-drained sandy loam or loamy soils preferred.
  • Tolerates poor, saline, and alkaline soils.
  • pH: 6.0 to 7.5 for best results.

4. Land Preparation

  • Ploughing 2–3 times to make a fine tilth.
  • Remove weeds and previous crop residues.
  • Add well-decomposed FYM or compost.

5. Seed Selection and Treatment

  • Use high-yielding, disease-resistant hybrid varieties.
  • Seed rate: 4–5 kg per hectare.
  • Seed treatment with Thiram or Bavistin to prevent fungal infections.

6. Sowing Methods

  • Line sowing or broadcasting methods used.
  • Row spacing: 45 cm; Plant spacing: 10–15 cm.
  • Sowing depth: 2–3 cm.

7. Irrigation Requirements

  • Minimal irrigation required; usually rainfed.
  • One or two supplemental irrigations if needed during flowering and grain filling.
  • Avoid waterlogging.

9. Pest and Disease Management

Pest/DiseaseSymptomsControl Measures
Downy Mildew White growth on leaves, stunted growth Fungicide spray (Metalaxyl) and resistant varieties
Smut Black powdery spores on ear heads Seed treatment + crop rotation
Shoot Fly Dead hearts in young plants Systemic insecticides during early stages

10. Harvesting

  • Harvest when grains are hard and dry.
  • Usually ready in 75–90 days depending on variety.
  • Manual cutting or sickles used.

11. Post-Harvest Management

  • Dry ear heads in sun to reduce grain moisture to 12–13%.
  • Thresh manually or by mechanical threshers.
  • Store in airtight containers or bags in dry, cool places.

12. Yield

Hybrid bajra varieties yield 25–35 quintals/ha; traditional varieties yield 15–20 quintals/ha.
